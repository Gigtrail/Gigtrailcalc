## Styling rules

### Background
- use a parchment or aged-paper style background
- subtle texture is okay if lightweight
- avoid flat stark white
- avoid black/dark mode as default

### Cards
- warm paper cards or soft cream panels
- rounded corners
- light borders
- subtle depth
- slightly inset vintage look okay
- no glassmorphism

### Typography
- headings should feel bold and vintage-inspired
- body text should remain highly readable
- preserve usability over style
- if using Google Fonts, choose something that fits the brand:
  - headings: a slab serif or bold serif vibe
  - body: clean readable sans-serif or humanist serif
- examples of acceptable directions:
  - heading style similar to Roboto Slab, Bitter, Arvo, or similar
  - body style similar to Inter, Source Sans, Lato, or similar

### Buttons
Primary buttons:
- burnt orange or dusty gold
- dark text or cream text depending on contrast
- sturdy, tactile feel
- hover state slightly darker

Secondary buttons:
- cream or paper tone with charcoal border
- subtle vintage feel

Danger buttons:
- muted rust red, not bright modern red

### Inputs and forms
- cream/paper input backgrounds
- warm dark text
- borders in muted brown/grey
- focus state in burnt orange
- placeholders in muted brown-grey
- forms should feel tactile and analogue-inspired but still modern
