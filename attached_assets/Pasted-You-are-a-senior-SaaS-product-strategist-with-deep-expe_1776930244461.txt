You are a senior SaaS product strategist with deep experience in early-stage product validation and user behavior.

I am building a touring calculator app for musicians called GigTrail.

I want you to critically evaluate whether the following feature should be included in ALPHA — not beta, not later — alpha.

FEATURE:
A dashboard section called “After the Show” that prompts users to log post-show actuals for past gigs.

The purpose is to:
- capture real-world outcomes (attendance, income, expenses)
- compare projected vs actual performance
- build venue intelligence over time
- improve future booking and pricing decisions

IMPORTANT CONTEXT:
- Users are working musicians (time-poor, tired, often on the road)
- The core product is a pre-show calculator (this is the main value)
- This feature is optional and should NOT interrupt the calculator flow
- It appears only after a show date has passed
- It lives on the dashboard as a lightweight memory prompt, not a task list
- Users can skip via “no data”
- The entire flow must take under 2 minutes on mobile
- No notifications in alpha (dashboard only)

V1 SCOPE:
Dashboard:
- Section: “After the Show”
- Lists past shows missing actuals
- Each item: venue name, date, “How’d it go?”
- Actions: “Add actuals” or “No data”

Form fields:
- attendance
- ticket income
- other income
- expenses
- play again? (yes / no / maybe)
- notes

Auto-calculated:
- actual net
- variance vs projected

Venue payoff:
- after 2 entries, show:
  - avg attendance
  - avg net
  - play again rate

GOAL OF ALPHA:
Validate BEHAVIOR, not polish.

Specifically:
- Do users actually log post-show data?
- Does this feel helpful or like admin?
- Does it increase perceived value of the app?

YOUR JOB:
Give me a brutally honest assessment.

Answer these:

1. Should this feature be included in alpha, or does it risk diluting focus?
2. What is the biggest reason users will ignore or abandon this feature?
3. What is the minimum version that still validates the idea?
4. What is the ONE thing that would make this feel valuable immediately?
5. What is the biggest UX mistake I could make here?
6. What metric should I track to determine if this is working?
7. If you had to simplify this even further, what would you cut?
8. If this fails in alpha, what is the most likely reason?

Be direct. Do not validate the idea unless it earns it.
Focus on real-world behavior, not theory.
