Add a clear, user-facing “Privacy & Data Use” section to Gig Trail that explains how touring, deal, and venue data is handled.

GOAL

Build trust with users by clearly explaining how their data is used, stored, and protected — especially given the sensitive nature of deal and income information.

-----------------------------------
CREATE SETTINGS SECTION
-----------------------------------

Add a new section:
"Privacy & Data"

This should be human-readable, not legal-heavy.

-----------------------------------
CONTENT REQUIREMENTS
-----------------------------------

Include clear sections:

1. Data Privacy
- "Your tour data, deals, and venue history are private to your account."
- "We do not share your individual data with other users."

2. Aggregated Insights
- "We may use anonymised, aggregated data to improve insights such as average fees or venue performance."
- "This data is never linked to you personally."

3. No Raw Data Sharing
- "We never expose your specific deal details to other users or venues."

4. User Control
- "You can edit or delete your data at any time."

5. Purpose
- "Your data helps you track performance and make better touring decisions."

-----------------------------------
LIGHTWEIGHT UI INTEGRATION
-----------------------------------

Add small trust indicators in key areas:

- Onboarding:
  "Your data stays private and is used to improve your own touring insights."

- Venue / Past Shows:
  Tooltip or helper text:
  "Your deal history is private to you."

-----------------------------------
UX REQUIREMENTS
-----------------------------------

- Keep tone simple and human
- Avoid legal jargon
- Keep it reassuring but honest
- Do not overwhelm the user

-----------------------------------
ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA
-----------------------------------

- Users can easily understand how their data is used
- Clear distinction between private data and aggregated insights
- Trust messaging appears in key parts of the product
- No implication that user data is publicly visible
